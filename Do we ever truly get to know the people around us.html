Do we ever truly get to know the people around us?

Published on November 28, 2025

In a world increasingly defined by digital connections and fleeting interactions, the question of whether we can ever truly know the people around us looms larger than ever. We scroll through social media profiles, exchange pleasantries at work, and share meals with family, yet beneath the surface, layers of complexity often remain unexplored.

Human relationships are built on a foundation of assumptions and projections. We see what we want to see, shaped by our own experiences and biases. A colleague's quiet demeanor might be interpreted as shyness, when in reality, it could stem from deep-seated anxiety or cultural conditioning. A friend's enthusiasm for certain topics might mask insecurities or past traumas that they've learned to hide behind a facade of confidence.

The challenge lies in the fact that true understanding requires vulnerability from both sides. It demands time, patience, and a willingness to look beyond the surface. Yet, in our fast-paced society, we often settle for superficial connections that satisfy our immediate social needs without delving deeper.

Technology, while connecting us globally, has paradoxically made genuine connection more elusive. We know more about celebrities and strangers online than we do about the people sitting next to us at dinner. The curated versions of ourselves we present on social media create barriers to authentic interaction.

But there is hope. True knowing comes from shared experiences, honest conversations, and the courage to be seen for who we really are. It requires active listening, empathy, and the recognition that everyone carries their own invisible burdens and joys.

Perhaps we never fully know anyone, including ourselves. But in the pursuit of understanding, in the moments of genuine connection we do achieve, we find meaning and humanity. The journey toward knowing others is, in many ways, a journey toward knowing ourselves better.
